Frequently Asked Questions

What is Trees Ranch?

Trees Ranch at Zion is one of the most significant properties on the Colorado Plateau and in all of the American southwest. Trees Ranch is considered by many to be a “private national park” because of its stunning viewshed, privacy and seclusion, and its proximity to Zion National Park.

Upon entering Trees Ranch you instantly know you’re in a very special place. Everything here was created by nature—thus our goal is simple: to not interfere, and let the land tell the story.

The property features a rich history with dozens of Native American sites and a pioneer ghost town dating back to 1861. The ranch is also regarded as a wildlife sanctuary.

The beauty and undisturbed sight-lines here are unparalleled. Trees Ranch is more than a destination; it’s a journey into the soul of nature.

What is the Grand Zion at Trees Ranch?

In 2025 a new low-density, experiential luxury hotel project was approved at Trees Ranch. The Grand Zion concept is what can be termed a “Landscape Hotel”—these hotels are built light on the land and feature undisturbed views.

The Grand Zion is currently anticipated to have 28 experiential hotel units: 17 Wilderness Portal pods and 11 luxury Stargazer Tents.

The resort will have two immersive dining experiences which will feature locally grown and sourced foods. A wellness center (spa) is also planned.

Will Trees Ranch get over-developed?

Above all, we value preservation and conservation of the land. Our goal is not to maximize development of the ranch. Our main objective is preserving this magical place and the wilderness feel of the property.

Reflecting a deep respect for the environment, less than 2% of the 2,085 acre property is planned to be developed, with no structures to be built next to the national park boundary or sensitive sites.

We’ve shown incredible restraint in planning only 28 experiential units on roughly 30 acres of the property. That equates to roughly 1 unit for every 75 acres of the ranch. The hotel will be incredibly light on the land with over 98% of the property preserved as open space.

An added bonus— the hotel is extremely well hidden: the location for the hotel lies tucked away on a hidden mesa at the back of the property. Thus it can’t be seen from the main highway that runs through town, and it is also hidden from most areas of the ranch itself.

When will the hotel open?

We are still early in the process, but current plans call for a 2028 hotel opening.

Will this be the nicest hotel in the Greater Zion region?

That is up to the guests to determine. We do believe we have a magical site that will open up the Zion experience for a new breed of traveler who is looking to experience the magic of Zion— but break away from the crowds of Zion National Park.

At the Grand Zion landscape hotel, you get your own private Zion. Unlike the park, we have no crowds, yet we still enjoy the same amazing scenery as Zion. Red-rock cliffs surround the property rising over 2,500 feet above the ranch. The ranch also features a private lake for our guests— the only large body of water near Zion Canyon.

Does Hollywood really come and shoot on Trees Ranch?

Trees Ranch offers rugged beauty that is unmatched on almost any private piece of land in the American Southwest. The unique topography of the ranch has not gone unnoticed from Hollywood: several movies have been shot here on location and a new major movie project is planned to be filmed on the property in 2025.
